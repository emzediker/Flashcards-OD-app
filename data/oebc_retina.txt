Retina – Diabetic Retinopathy
8 flashcards

Leading cause of blindness in adults aged 20–64 in the United States?
1 of 8

Diabetic retinopathy; strongest risk factor is duration of diabetes, followed by poor glycemic control.

What defines mild nonproliferative diabetic retinopathy (NPDR)?
2 of 8

Presence of microaneurysms only.

What defines moderate NPDR?
3 of 8

More than mild NPDR but less than severe NPDR; does not meet 4-2-1 rule.

What is the 4-2-1 rule for severe NPDR?
4 of 8

4 quadrants of hemorrhages/microaneurysms, 2 quadrants of venous beading, or 1 quadrant of IRMA.

What defines proliferative diabetic retinopathy (PDR)?
5 of 8

Presence of neovascularization (NVD and/or NVE).

What findings define high-risk PDR?
6 of 8

Greater than one-quarter disc diameter of NVD, or NVD/NVE with vitreous or preretinal hemorrhage.

What defines clinically significant macular edema (CSME)?
7 of 8

Retinal thickening within 500 microns of the fovea, or thickening greater than one disc diameter within one disc diameter of the fovea, or exudates within 500 microns of the fovea with thickening.

First-line treatment for diabetic macular edema?
8 of 8

Intravitreal anti-VEGF therapy; steroids may be used if poor response.

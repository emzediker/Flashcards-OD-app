Erythema Multiforme / Stevens-Johnson Syndrome / Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis
Erythema Multiforme / Stevens-Johnson Syndrome / Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis
1 of 32
Definition / Cause: Variants of the same disease spectrum involving a Type IV hypersensitivity reaction. Common triggers include medications (especially sulfonamides and anticonvulsants) and infections such as HSV or Mycoplasma.
Symptoms: Flu-like prodrome, red eyes, eye pain or burning, skin rash.
Signs: Target lesions, dusky macules, bullous skin lesions, conjunctival injection, symblepharon.
Treatment: Topical steroids for ocular involvement. Systemic management in conjunction with medical team.
Follow-Up: Daily.

Cicatricial Pemphigoid
Cicatricial Pemphigoid
2 of 32
Definition / Cause: Systemic autoimmune disease causing progressive mucocutaneous inflammation and scarring. Typically affects women over 55 years old.
Symptoms: Dryness, redness, foreign body sensation, tearing, burning, decreased vision, photophobia; usually bilateral.
Signs: Conjunctival inflammation, symblepharon, forniceal shortening, scarring.
Treatment: Systemic immunosuppressive therapy, oral prednisone, palliative ocular surface treatment.
Follow-Up: 1 week.

Superior Limbic Keratoconjunctivitis
Superior Limbic Keratoconjunctivitis
3 of 32
Definition / Cause: Chronic inflammatory condition caused by friction between the upper eyelid and superior bulbar conjunctiva; associated with dry eye, thyroid eye disease, rheumatoid arthritis, Sjögren syndrome, and contact lens wear.
Symptoms: Red eye, foreign body sensation, itching, mild photophobia, episodic flares.
Signs: Superior conjunctival thickening, radial injection, fine papillae, superior SPK, filaments.
Treatment: Mild—lubrication, cyclosporine or lifitegrast. Severe—autologous serum, tacrolimus 0.03%, acetylcysteine 10%, silver nitrate.
Follow-Up: Every 2 weeks until remission.

Subconjunctival Hemorrhage
Subconjunctival Hemorrhage
4 of 32
Definition / Cause: Bleeding beneath conjunctiva due to Valsalva, trauma, or anticoagulants.
Symptoms: Red eye without pain or vision loss.
Signs: Well-demarcated subconjunctival blood.
Treatment: None required.
Follow-Up: None; reassure.

Allergic Conjunctivitis
Allergic Conjunctivitis
5 of 32
Definition / Cause: IgE-mediated hypersensitivity to airborne allergens.
Symptoms: Itching, redness, watery eyes.
Signs: Chemosis, papillae, eyelid edema, no preauricular node.
Treatment: Artificial tears, antihistamine/mast cell stabilizers, short steroid course if severe.
Follow-Up: 2 weeks.

Cataracts – Overview
Cataracts – Overview
1 of 27
Definition: Lens opacity causing light scatter and reduced visual function; congenital or acquired.
Core Symptoms: Blur, glare/halos, night driving difficulty, reduced contrast; asymptomatic early.
Core Sign: Lens opacification on slit lamp; type/location helps determine etiology and symptoms.
Congenital Cataracts – Overview
Congenital Cataracts – Overview
2 of 27
Definition: Present at birth; severity ranges from minimal impact to severe visual deprivation leading to amblyopia or strabismus.
Symptoms: Often asymptomatic; infant may rub eye.
Signs: Leukocoria, strabismus, nystagmus; cataract morphology.
Treatment: Cataract surgery ASAP if visually significant.
Follow Up: Post-operative follow up at 1 day, 1 week, and 1 month.

Posterior Capsule Opacification
Posterior Capsule Opacification
21 of 27
Definition: Secondary cataract due to lens epithelial cell proliferation after PCIOL.
Symptoms: Blur, glare, halos, reduced night vision.
Signs: Elschnig pearls on posterior capsule.
Treatment: YAG laser capsulotomy.
Complications: CME, retinal detachment, IOP spike.
Aphakic or Pseudophakic Bullous Keratopathy
Aphakic or Pseudophakic Bullous Keratopathy
22 of 27
Cause: Endothelial failure following cataract surgery.
Symptoms: Pain, photophobia, foreign body sensation.
Signs: Corneal edema, bullae, Descemet folds.
Treatment: Hypertonic saline, IOP reduction; severe cases require PKP.

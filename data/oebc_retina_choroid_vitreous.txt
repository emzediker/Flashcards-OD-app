Moderate NPDR: More than mild less than sever

2 quadrants of venous beading
1 quadrant of IRMA
PDR: Presence of neovascularization
High Risk:
>1/4 DD of NVD
NVD with VH or preretinal heme
NVE with VH or preretinal heme
CSME:
Exudates within 500 um of fovea with thickening
Thickening > 1DD within 1 DD of fovea
Thickening within 500 um of fovea
Symptoms: Asymptomatic but may complain of blurry vision
Signs: See above
Treatment: Depends on the presentation
CME: Anti-VEGF followed by steroids if poor response
High Risk PDR: PRP
PDR (not high risk) w/ or w/o CME: Anti-VEGF
PPV Indications: Dense non clearing VH, tractional RD, ERMs or VMT with significant symptoms, dense premacular heme, chronic DME not responsive to treatment, severe neo and fibrous proliferation unresponsive to PRP/anti-VEGF

Mild NPDR: 6 months
Moderate/Severe NPDR: 4 months
PDR (not high risk): 2 months
High risk PDR: Monthly
Any CSME: Monthly

Ocular Ischemic Syndrome
12 of 51

Signs: Dilated (not tortuous) veins, narrowed arterioles, mid peripheral hemes, iris neo, neo of disc, neo of retina, collateral vessels on forehead, episcleral injection, CWS, CRAO may occur, retinal Mas
Treatment: Carotid doppler, treat neo with anti-VEGF and PRP, control HTN and high IOP if present
Follow Up: Refer immediately
